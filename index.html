What I'm building

In Development

Ari

An AI companion for elderly users — gentle, reliable, and genuinely warm.

The Problem

Many elderly people feel disconnected — from routines, from family, from themselves. Generic apps don't speak their language and require too much cognitive effort just to get started.

The Idea

A calm, conversational AI companion that sends gentle reminders, checks in daily, shares small motivations, and offers real emotional support — without being overwhelming or patronizing.

Current Status

Designing core flows and validating with target users. Early conversations with caregivers suggest strong resonance with the concept. Defining MVP scope and early technical approach while validating with users.

Philosophy

How I think

01

Simple over clever

If I have to explain how to use it, it's not ready. The best product experiences feel obvious in hindsight — which is the hardest thing to achieve.

02

Useful before beautiful

Design matters deeply — but it has to earn its place. I start with whether something actually works for a real person before thinking about how it looks.

03

Retention tells the truth

Anyone can get a download. The honest metric is whether someone came back the next day. Retention is where the real product feedback lives.

04

Products need to pay for themselves

I take the business side seriously. A product that doesn't sustain itself can't keep helping people. I think about pricing and monetization early — not as an afterthought.
